Introduction

Sustainable Development Goal 11 (SDG 11) is dedicated to making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. With over 55% of the global population currently living in urban areas, and an expected rise to 68% by 2050, sustainable urban planning is crucial to ensure a high quality of life. As cities expand, they face challenges such as overpopulation, inadequate housing, pollution, inefficient transport systems, and vulnerability to natural disasters. SDG 11 aims to tackle these issues through improved urban planning, infrastructure development, waste management, and climate adaptation strategies. By creating sustainable cities, we can enhance economic growth, social well-being, and environmental protection. This page explores the challenges, solutions, and success stories related to SDG 11 and how we can work together to build a sustainable future.

  1. 1. Affordable and Safe Housing

    • Governments must implement inclusive housing policies and support slum upgrading programs to improve living conditions.
    • Green buildings with energy-efficient designs help reduce environmental impact.

  2. 2. Green and Efficient Public Transport

    • Investing in public transport systems such as metros, buses, and cycling infrastructure reduces traffic congestion and pollution.
    • Promoting electric vehicles (EVs) and carpooling can significantly lower carbon emissions.

  3. 3. Smart Waste Management

    • Implementing waste segregation, recycling, and composting can reduce landfill overflow.
    • Waste-to-energy initiatives convert garbage into renewable energy sources.

  4. 4. Sustainable Energy and Green Buildings

    • Using solar, wind, and hydro energy can make cities more self-sufficient and sustainable.
    • Smart grids and energy-efficient architecture reduce power consumption.
